HOPS

18/01/24

Welcome, beer enthusiasts, to the heart of the brewing process, where botanical magic meets malty alchemy. In the world of craft beer, few ingredients are as celebrated and essential as hops.

What is hops?

Hops (Humulus lupulus) are the flowering cones of a perennial climbing plant that belongs to the Cannabaceae family. While hops have been used in brewing for centuries, it's the craft beer revolution that has truly brought them into the spotlight. The primary role of hops in beer is to impart bitterness, balance sweetness, and act as a natural preservative.

Varieties of hops

Just as grapes contribute different flavors to wines, various hop varieties lend unique characteristics to beers. From the citrusy notes of Cascade to the floral aromas of Saaz, each hop strain brings its own personality to the brewing process. Brewers carefully select hop varieties to craft a beer that appeals to a wide range of palates, resulting in the diverse array of IPAs, pale ales, and other hop-forward styles we enjoy today.

Usage

There are some beer styles where the usage of different hops are very important for the brew, such as: Indian Pale Ale (IPA) Pale Ale Amber Ales Brown Ales New England Pale Ale (NEIPA)
